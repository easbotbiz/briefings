# 2A Legal Intel — February 9, 2026

## Summary
ATF Director nominee Robert Cekada sailed through his Senate confirmation hearing with bipartisan support, pledging to protect 2A rights. The Fifth Circuit delivered a major win in *U.S. v. Hembree*, striking down a lifetime gun ban for a non-violent felon. SCOTUS continues to sit on cert petitions for large-capacity magazine, AR-15, and public transit carry cases while *Wolford v. Lopez* (Hawaii carry restrictions) was argued in January with conservative justices appearing skeptical of the state's position.

## NY State
- **Knight v. City of New York (2d Cir.)** — Second Circuit clarified standing for potential gun dealers challenging NYC regulations. Court ruled plaintiff had standing to challenge the city's place-of-business requirement for dealers but lacked standing on the 90-day purchase limit. Signals ongoing chipping away at NYC's regulatory thicket. [NY Law Journal](https://www.law.com/newyorklawjournal/2026/02/02/knight-v-city-of-new-york-standing-to-challenge-firearms-regulation/)
- **NYPD License Division Delays — New Federal Suit Filed** — Four Brooklyn/Queens plaintiffs filed suit arguing NYPD's 22-month concealed carry permit processing times violate the Second Amendment. State law sets a 6-month timeline. NYPD collected $340/application from thousands of post-*Bruen* applicants, then slow-walked processing. This is deliberate bureaucratic resistance to *Bruen*. [NY1](https://ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/politics/2026/01/22/nypd-license-division-concealed-carry-delays)
- **Mancuso v. New York (SCOTUS No. 25-356)** — New York filed its brief in opposition to cert petition challenging NY felon-in-possession statutes (Penal Law §§ 265.03(3) and 265.02(1)) under the Second Amendment. On appeal from NY's highest court. [Duke Firearms Law Center](https://firearmslaw.duke.edu/2026/02/scotus-watch-2-2-other-developments)

## Federal

Declared: "ATF's mission is not to burden lawful gun owners or undermine the Second Amendment." NSSF, FOP both endorsed. Even Sen. Booker (D-NJ) expressed relief at the nomination. On suppressors, Cekada told Sen. Mike Lee that if Congress removes suppressors from NFA, ATF would fully support it. Likely to be confirmed. [The Reload](https://thereload.com/trump-atf-director-nominee-faces-little-pushback-in-confirmation-hearing/) · [Bearing Arms](https://bearingarms.com/camedwards/2026/02/04/trumps-atf-nominee-faces-grilling-from-senate-judiciary-n1231449)

- **NFA Background Checks Explode — "Year of the Suppressor"** — NSSF reports NFA item background checks hit 206,000+ in January 2026, up 121% year-over-year following removal of the $200 NFA tax. Virginia pushing a $500 state-level suppressor tax as counter-move. [Colion Noir](https://www.mrcolionnoir.com/nssf-background-checks-for-nfa-items-exploded-in-january/)

## Court Watch
- **United States v. Hembree (5th Cir.)** — MAJOR WIN. Unanimous 3-0 panel struck down lifetime gun ban under § 922(g)(1) as applied to defendant with a single non-violent drug possession felony. Court found no historical analogue supporting permanent disarmament for non-violent conduct. Deepens circuit split — some circuits still uphold blanket felon bans. This case is one of the cleanest vehicles for eventual SCOTUS review. [AmmoLand](https://www.ammoland.com/2026/02/major-2a-win-fifth-circuit-strikes-down-lifetime-gun-ban-for-non-violent-felon/)
- **Wolford v. Lopez (SCOTUS — argued Jan 20)** — Hawaii's "default ban" on public carry challenged. Conservative justices appeared skeptical of Hawaii's position during oral argument. Decision expected by June. If SCOTUS rules broadly, it could impact NY CCIA sensitive-place restrictions. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-supreme-court-hear-challenge-hawaii-handgun-limits-2026-01-20/) · [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jan/20/supreme-court-hawaii-gun-law-case)
- **U.S. v. Hemani (SCOTUS)** — Marijuana user gun ban case. FPC filed amicus arguing Congress lacks Article I authority to enact the ban at all (bypassing 2A question). Trump admin + 20 AGs (19 Dem + 1 Rep) defend prohibition. CO Gov. Polis broke with his own AG to publicly support gun rights for marijuana users. Decision expected early summer. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/congress-cant-enact-sweeping-gun-prohibitions-argues-fpc-in-new-supreme-court-brief)
- **Knife Rights v. Bonta (9th Cir.)** — Unanimous panel upheld California's switchblade ban under *Bruen* historical tradition test. Court found enough historical analogues to justify prohibition on concealed carry of switchblades. Disappointing but narrow — facial challenge only. [Colion Noir](https://www.mrcolionnoir.com/appeals-court-states-may-criminalize-possession-of-certain-knives/)
- **Koons v. Platkin (3d Cir. — en banc)** — Third Circuit granted full-court review of FPC's challenge to New Jersey's post-*Bruen* carry restrictions. Prior panel decision vacated. NJ's sweeping sensitive-place regime will get full scrutiny. Directly relevant to NY CCIA. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/third-circuit-grants-full-court-review-in-fpc-challenge-to-new-jersey-carry-ban-scheme)
- **SCOTUS Cert Watch** — Still awaiting orders on large-capacity magazine ban, AR-15 ban (*Viramontes v. Cook County*), and public transit carry (*Schoenthal v. Raoul*) petitions — all relisted multiple times. *Thompson v. U.S.* and *Vincent v. Bondi* (felon possession) still pending after Jan 26 mass denial of similar petitions. Possible dissent from denial being drafted. [Duke Center](https://firearmslaw.duke.edu/2026/02/scotus-watch-2-2-other-developments)
- **Elite Precision Customs v. ATF (5th Cir.)** — FPC filed opening brief challenging federal ban on interstate handgun sales. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/fpc-files-fifth-circuit-brief-in-lawsuit-challenging-federal-ban-on-interstate-handgun-sales)
- **First Circuit: Granata v. Campbell** — U.S. government filed amicus in *support* of FPC's challenge to Massachusetts handgun ban. Trump DOJ siding with gun owners against a state ban. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/united-states-files-brief-in-support-of-fpcs-challenge-to-massachusetts-handgun-ban)

## Gunny's Assessment
The 2A legal landscape is moving fast on multiple fronts. *Hembree* is the Fifth Circuit doing *Bruen* right — expect SCOTUS to eventually take a felon-possession case and this one's a clean vehicle. Meanwhile, SCOTUS is sitting on mag bans and AWB cert petitions like they're waiting for something — possibly *Wolford* to come down first to establish the broader framework. NY continues its passive-aggressive compliance strategy: technically issuing permits while slow-walking everything to 22 months. The NYPD suit is overdue. Cekada at ATF is the best news gun owners have had from that agency in a generation — a career law enforcement guy who actually says the quiet part out loud about not targeting lawful owners. Watch Virginia's $500 suppressor tax — if that passes, other blue states will copy it as the new end-run around federal NFA reform.
