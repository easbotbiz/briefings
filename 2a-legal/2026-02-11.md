# 2A Legal Intel — 11 Feb 2026

## Summary
Virginia's Democrat-controlled House passed a sweeping gun control package including an AWB, magazine caps, and sensitive-place restrictions — headed to the Senate and Gov. Spanberger's desk. ATF Director nominee Robert Cekada sailed through his Senate confirmation hearing with bipartisan support, pledging not to burden lawful gun owners. At the federal level, SCOTUS is sitting on *Wolford v. Lopez* (Hawaii carry restrictions) after Jan 20 oral arguments, and *U.S. v. Hemani* (marijuana user gun ban) is drawing amicus briefs from an unusual NRA-NORML alliance. New York is among five states pushing firearms excise tax legislation.

## NY State
- **NY Firearms Excise Tax Bill Introduced** — New York is one of five states (with MD, MA, VA, WA) that have introduced excise tax bills targeting firearms and ammunition manufacturers/dealers this session, following the California/Colorado model from 2024. [Bloomberg Law](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/daily-tax-report-state/guns-and-ammo-excise-taxes-getting-attention-in-five-states)
- **NY State Bar Association Backs Lost/Stolen Reporting** — NYSBA backed an ABA resolution calling for laws requiring gun owners to promptly report missing firearms under threat of civil or criminal penalties. [NYSBA](https://nysba.org/new-york-state-bar-association-is-a-tireless-advocate-for-the-rule-of-law/)
- **CCIA Status** — No new legislative or judicial developments on the Concealed Carry Improvement Act this cycle. The Third Circuit's en banc review in *Koons v. Platkin* (NJ's parallel post-Bruen carry scheme) granted Dec 2025 remains the most analogous active case. NY's CCIA restrictions remain in effect.

## Federal
- **Freedom From Unfair Gun Taxes Act** — Sen. Jim Risch (R-ID) with Reps. Darrel Issa (R-CA) and Richard Hudson (R-NC) introduced legislation to prohibit states from imposing punitive excise taxes on firearms, ammo, and components. Targets CA's existing 11% tax and CO's 6.5% tax, plus pending bills in five more states including NY. [NSSF](https://www.nssf.org/articles/freedom-from-unfair-gun-taxes-act-aims-to-stop-punishing-taxes/) | [Colion Noir](https://www.mrcolionnoir.com/federal-proposal-would-eliminate-sin-taxes-on-firearms-and-accessories/)
- **GOA Pushes Concealed Carry Reciprocity** — GOA is pushing to attach the Constitutional Concealed Carry Reciprocity Act to the upcoming DHS funding bill, citing Schumer's recent "shall not be infringed" statement. DHS funding expires in ~2 weeks. [GOA](https://www.gunowners.org/na020326/)
- **U.S. Attorney Pirro (D.C.) Threatens Gun Owners** — Jeanine Pirro declared she'll jail anyone carrying in D.C. regardless of out-of-state permits, drawing sharp rebukes from GOA and Cato Institute for contradicting DOJ's own pro-2A positions. [Colion Noir](https://www.mrcolionnoir.com/shock-us-attorney-to-incarcerate-law-abiding-gun-owners-entering-dc/)
- **NH Campus Carry (HB 1793) Advances** — Passed the NH House, headed to Senate. Would eliminate campus carry bans at public colleges and includes a $10K statutory damage provision against non-compliant institutions. [GOA](https://www.gunowners.org/nh02052026/)

## Court Watch
- **Wolford v. Lopez (SCOTUS)** — Oral arguments held Jan 20, 2026. Challenges Hawaii's "default ban" requiring express permission to carry on publicly accessible private property. Conservative justices appeared sympathetic; suggested Hawaii was singling out firearms vs. other rights. Decision expected by end of term (June 2026). [SCOTUSblog](https://www.scotusblog.com/2026/01/supreme-court-appears-sympathetic-to-gun-owners-challenge-to-hawaii-law/)
- **United States v. Hemani (SCOTUS)** — Challenges federal ban on gun possession by marijuana users (§922(g)(3)). Amicus briefs filed by NRA, NORML, FPC, NYSRPA, SAF, Drug Policy Alliance — unusual left-right coalition. FPC argues Congress lacks Article I authority for the ban entirely. Oral arguments pending. [Reason](https://reason.com/2026/02/02/the-nra-and-norml-unite-to-oppose-the-federal-gun-ban-for-marijuana-users/) | [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/congress-cant-enact-sweeping-gun-prohibitions-argues-fpc-in-new-supreme-court-brief)
- **SCOTUS Denies Cert on Felon-in-Possession Cases** — Court declined review in *Duarte v. United States*, *Zherka v. Bondi*, *Collins v. Bondi*, and *Pierre v. United States* — all challenging §922(g)(1) lifetime bans for felons. For now, the felon disarmament question stays with the circuits. [SCOTUSblog](https://www.scotusblog.com/2026/01/supreme-court-denies-review-in-several-gun-cases/)
- **Elite Precision Customs v. ATF (5th Circuit)** — FPC filed opening brief Feb 4 challenging federal ban on interstate handgun sales by licensed dealers. Appealing district court's adverse ruling. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/fpc-files-fifth-circuit-brief-in-lawsuit-challenging-federal-ban-on-interstate-handgun-sales)
- **Koons v. Platkin (3rd Circuit En Banc)** — Full Third Circuit will review NJ's post-Bruen carry restrictions after Dec 2025 en banc grant. Panel decision upholding key elements was vacated. Directly relevant to NY CCIA analysis. [FPC](http://www.firearmspolicy.org/third-circuit-grants-full-court-review-in-fpc-challenge-to-new-jersey-carry-ban-scheme)
- **4th Circuit: As-Applied §922(g)(9) Challenge** — Court held that district courts must consider as-applied Second Amendment challenges to the domestic violence misdemeanor gun ban under the Bruen framework, not just facial challenges. [VA Lawyers Weekly](https://valawyersweekly.com/2026/02/04/criminal-district-court-must-consider-defendants-as-applied-challenge-to-%C2%A7-922g9/)

## Gunny's Assessment
Virginia is the main event right now — if that AWB clears the Senate and Spanberger signs it, expect an immediate FPC/GOA/NRA legal blitz that'll make the post-Bruen NY litigation look like a warm-up. Meanwhile, *Wolford v. Lopez* is the SCOTUS case to watch — a ruling striking Hawaii's carry scheme would ripple straight into NY's CCIA sensitive-places framework. And Cekada at ATF is genuinely good news; the industry and enforcement community are aligned on this one for once. NY's firearms excise tax push is the quiet threat — death by a thousand taxes instead of outright bans.
