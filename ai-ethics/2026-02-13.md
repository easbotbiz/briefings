## Academia & Research

- **Governing Algorithmic Discrimination: Anti-Discrimination Law Isn't Enough** — Prof. Pauline Kim (Washington University) argues in a new paper that existing anti-discrimination law can't address bias hidden in algorithms because it was designed for individual acts of bias, not structural group-level harms from pattern recognition systems. Employers use AI across hiring, monitoring, and promotions — and when third-party vendors supply the tools, accountability gets even murkier. ## 🚨 Red Flags

- **Goldman Sachs explicitly stating they'll "constrain headcount growth" as AI agents take over accounting and compliance.** This isn't speculation anymore — it's a Fortune 50 bank's stated strategy. The "digital co-worker" framing is doing a lot of heavy lifting to avoid saying "replacement."
- **Training data poisoning at $60/attack and 56% prompt injection success rates.** We're deploying these systems into critical infrastructure while the security model is fundamentally broken. The ZDNET piece makes clear: there are no known fixes for several of these vulnerabilities.
- **Trump's EO creating a litigation task force specifically to sue states that regulate AI.** Using federal power to actively dismantle consumer protections while offering nothing in return. Congress keeps saying no (99-1!), so the executive branch is going around them.
- **AI agents bypassing MFA via deepfake injection into camera feeds.** Authentication systems weren't designed for this threat model. We're in "the locks don't work anymore" territory.

## 💡 Bright Spots

- **Congress holding the line on state AI regulation** — the 99-1 vote rejecting federal preemption of state AI laws is remarkable bipartisan consensus that local regulation matters.
- **King's College cardiac imaging bias fix** — proving that racial bias in medical AI can be addressed with relatively simple training adjustments, without sacrificing accuracy.
- **ASEAN's agentic AI governance framework** — five nations collaborating on governance specifically for autonomous agents. Someone's thinking ahead.
- **Anthropic putting real money behind regulation** — $20M is serious. Whether you agree with their approach or not, an AI company lobbying *for* its own regulation is structurally healthier than the alternative.

## 📄 Paper of the Day

**"How Should AI Safety Benchmarks Benchmark Safety?"** — A comprehensive review of 210 AI safety benchmarks that documents systematic failures in how we measure AI safety. The authors argue that current benchmarks suffer from fundamental epistemic and technical shortcomings, and propose a framework grounded in established risk management principles and measurement theory. Includes a practical checklist for developing robust safety benchmarks. If you're evaluating AI safety claims, this is the paper that tells you why most safety scores are less meaningful than they appear.

[arXiv:2601.23112v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.23112v2)
