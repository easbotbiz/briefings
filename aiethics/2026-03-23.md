## 📄 Paper of the Week

**EvoJail: Evolving Jailbreaks — Automated Multi-Objective Long-Tail Attacks on Large Language Models** — Researchers present EvoJail, a framework that uses multi-objective evolutionary search to automatically discover novel jailbreak strategies against LLMs. Instead of relying on handcrafted rules, the system jointly maximizes attack effectiveness while minimizing output perplexity (making attacks harder to detect). It introduces a "semantic-algorithmic solution representation" that captures both high-level intent and low-level encryption-decryption logic. The framework integrates LLM-assisted mutation and crossover operators, meaning it uses AI to evolve attacks against AI. Results show it consistently discovers diverse, effective jailbreak strategies competitive with existing methods. **Why it matters:** This is an arms race accelerator. Automated jailbreak discovery means safety teams can no longer rely on patching known attack patterns — the attack surface is evolving faster than manual red-teaming can keep up. [arXiv:2603.20122](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20122)

## 🚨 Red Flags

- **Project Maven's AI targeting now active in Iran operations.** The book excerpt confirms what was suspected: AI is making lethal targeting decisions in active combat. The "theoretical" phase is over. Human oversight in the targeting loop remains an open question.
- **DoorDash gig workers training their own replacements.** The Tasks app is a dystopian feedback loop — precarious workers generating training data for the robots and AI systems that will eliminate their jobs. Blocked in worker-protection states. That tells you everything.
- **ChatGPT "Adult Mode" creates the ultimate honeypot.** Sexual behavior data is among the most sensitive information possible. OpenAI's memory feature + erotica = a surveillance database that could be weaponized through breaches, subpoenas, or authoritarian government demands.
- **Zuckerberg building an AI CEO agent.** When the person running a company with 3+ billion users starts delegating decision-making to AI, the question of who's actually governing becomes urgent.

## 💡 Bright Spots

- **Moxie Marlinspike bringing encryption to AI conversations.** The Confer-Meta collaboration could be as transformative for AI privacy as Signal Protocol was for messaging. If billions of Meta AI users get end-to-end encryption by default, it would be the single biggest privacy upgrade in AI history.
- **Anthropic's sworn declaration on no kill switch.** Whatever you think of the Anthropic-Pentagon dispute, a company putting in writing — under penalty of perjury — that it has no ability to remotely disable military AI systems is a meaningful accountability marker.
- **Game developers collectively refusing AI at GDC.** The gaming industry's grassroots resistance to AI remains one of the most organized consumer-driven pushbacks against uncritical AI adoption. Developers are voting with their projects.
- **Nate B Jones' "contextual stewardship" framework.** Finally, a clear articulation of what humans uniquely bring to AI-augmented work: institutional memory, organizational judgment, and the ability to know what the AI doesn't know it doesn't know. This should be required reading for every executive making AI deployment decisions.
