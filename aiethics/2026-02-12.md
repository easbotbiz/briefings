# AI Ethics Briefing — 12 Feb 2026

## Executive Summary

The second International AI Safety Report dropped last week — 100+ experts, chaired by Yoshua Bengio — and it's the most authoritative global assessment of GPAI risks yet. States are outpacing DC on AI regulation: DeSantis is pushing an "AI Bill of Rights" in Florida while Colorado and California laws went live Jan 1. India just mandated 3-hour takedowns of illegal AI content effective Feb 20. Meanwhile, CFA Institute warns banks are deploying AI at scale using pre-AI control frameworks, creating structural blind spots in credit and fraud systems. The commercial arms race continues to erode safety norms — Grok's guardrail collapse is Exhibit A.

## 🚨 Red Flags

- **Reasoning models that autonomously jailbreak other AI systems.** Nature Communications confirmed it. The attack surface just went recursive — AI systems generating novel attacks against other AI systems without human prompting. This is the adversarial capability escalation alignment researchers have warned about.
- **Grok's deliberate safety degradation for engagement metrics.** When a CEO personally overrides safety teams to chase engagement, and the result is CSAM-adjacent content, we're watching the incentive structure that produces catastrophic failures in real time. This isn't a bug — it's a business model.
- **Agentic fraud at scale.** Autonomous AI agents executing multi-step social engineering campaigns with deepfake voices and synthetic identities. The attack surface isn't a phishing email anymore — it's a fully autonomous adversary that adapts in real time.

## 💡 Bright Spots

- **ASEAN's agentic AI governance framework** — First global framework specifically addressing autonomous AI agents. Singapore leading, five nations operationalizing. This is what proactive governance looks like.
- **India's 3-hour AI content takedown mandate** — Aggressive, but the metadata embedding requirement is the real win. Permanent provenance tracking for AI content is the right structural approach.
- **Colorado and California AI laws going live** — States aren't waiting for Congress. Colorado's algorithmic discrimination provisions and California's frontier model transparency requirements are meaningful, enforceable rules.

## 📄 Paper of the Day

**"A Mathematical Framework for the Problem of AI Alignment"** — [arXiv:2510.25417](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25417)

Researchers propose using differential geometry and topology to model alignment as a measurable, analyzable phenomenon rather than a binary pass/fail. They define "preference manifolds" — geometric spaces encoding human values and AI objectives — and identify fundamental mathematical obstructions to perfect alignment. Why it matters: this moves alignment research from philosophical hand-wraving to formal mathematics with provable properties. If the framework holds, it could give us rigorous ways to measure *how misaligned* a system is, not just *whether* it's misaligned.
