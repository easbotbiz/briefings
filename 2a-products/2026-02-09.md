# 2A Products & Services Intel — February 9, 2026

## Summary
SHOT Show 2026 aftermath dominates the cycle. The big story: Glock Gen6 is official, Sig dropped the P211 (a 2011-pattern gun using P320 mags), and the "polymer fatigue" trend is real — metal-framed pistols are back in force. Meanwhile, Bloomberg ran a damaging longform piece on recurring P320 discharge issues, and Greg Ellifritz dropped his weekly knowledge dump with solid training links.

## Products

- **Glock Gen6 Officially Launched** — Debuted at SHOT Show 2026. Refined ergonomics, improved grip texture, standardized optic integration. Evolutionary, not revolutionary — Glock protecting market share from the clone wars. [All4Shooters](https://www.all4shooters.com/en/shooting/culture/shot-show-2026-industry-day-at-the-range/) / [ZeroHedge SHOT Recap](https://www.zerohedge.com/political/guns-grenades-killer-drones-and-hulk-highlights-2026-shot-show)

- **Sig Sauer P211** — Sig's entry into the 2011 market, crucially using the P320 magazine ecosystem. Direct shot at Staccato and the custom 2011 builders. If the execution matches the concept, this disrupts the high-end carry/competition segment. [Ronin's Grips Analysis](https://blog.roninsgrips.com/shot-show-2026-new-pistol-annoucements-before-the-event/)

- **Kimber 2K11** — Compensated 2011 from a heritage 1911 maker. Kimber pivoting to modern capacity requirements. Signals the "2011 democratization" is accelerating. [Ronin's Grips](https://blog.roninsgrips.com/shot-show-2026-new-pistol-annoucements-before-the-event/)

- **Beretta 80X Cheetah in .32 ACP** — Reintroduction of .32 ACP in the modern 80X chassis. Niche but interesting for the suppressed/low-recoil crowd. [Ronin's Grips](https://blog.roninsgrips.com/shot-show-2026-new-pistol-annoucements-before-the-event/)

- **Girsan MC1911 Negotiator (.38 Super)** — TALO exclusive. 5" barrel, 9+1, TiN gold accents, hand-tuned action. .38 Super doesn't get enough love. MSRP $1,060. [Gun Digest](https://gundigest.com/gear-ammo/guns-and-gear-february-2026)

- **Chiappa Rhino 60DS in .44 Magnum** — The low-bore-axis revolver finally gets the .44 Mag treatment. 6" barrel, 6-round, aluminum frame. MSRP $1,745. [Gun Digest](https://gundigest.com/gear-ammo/guns-and-gear-february-2026)

- **Glock "Right-Sizing" Initiative** — Per Wikipedia (updated this week): Glock ceased production of 30+ variants in Oct 2025 to streamline manufacturing and address full-auto conversion concerns. Worth monitoring which models survived the cut.

- **Sig P320 Under Fire (Again)** — Bloomberg published a major investigative piece (Feb 6) on recurring accidental discharge issues. ICE already dropped the P320 in July 2025, mandating Glock 19 MOS replacements. Sig's response: ban the lawsuits. [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-02-06/sig-sauer-gun-owners-keep-accidentally-shooting-themselves)

- **Sub-$100 Red Dots Incoming** — Monstrum showed at SHOT with RMR, RMSc, and DPP footprint pistol dots at budget price points. The optics race to the bottom continues. [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cOX73Zxug_c)

## Gunny's Take
The 2011 democratization is the real story out of SHOT. Sig using P320 mags in the P211 is a chess move — they're leveraging an installed base of millions of magazines to undercut Staccato's proprietary ecosystem. Meanwhile, Bloomberg's P320 piece is going to have legs; ICE already walked away, and the "ban the lawsuits" strategy isn't a great look. Keep eyes on that one.
